## Raise for a milestone, not for a runway
"Raise to reach a specific milestone, not just to survive longer." John Doe wants each round tied to
a concrete proof point the next investor will pay for.

## Smart money beats cheap money
"The cheapest term sheet can be the most expensive one." He prefers investors who bring customers and
hiring help over a slightly higher valuation from a passive check.

## Dilution is a tool, not a sin
He is not dogmatic about ownership: "Giving up more of a bigger thing is fine." The question is
whether the capital actually accelerates a working machine.
